# Project Hope Lynden Inventory Management Improvement

## Executive Summary and Discovery Cover Sheet

**Prepared for:** Project Hope Food Bank of Lynden  
**Prepared by:** Scott McIntosh  
**Project:** Inventory Management System Review and Improvement  
**Status:** Discovery and planning

## Purpose

This project is a volunteer effort to help Project Hope Food Bank of Lynden review its current inventory management process and identify practical improvements that fit the organization's budget, staffing, and day-to-day workflow.

The goal is not to replace the current Excel-based process simply for the sake of replacing it. The first goal is to understand what is working today, where the process is creating friction, and what level of system improvement would provide the most value without becoming difficult or expensive to maintain.

## Current Situation

Project Hope currently uses Excel spreadsheets to manage inventory. That approach has the advantage of being familiar and inexpensive, but spreadsheets can become difficult to maintain as inventory grows, reporting needs expand, and multiple people need access to accurate information.

This review will look at how inventory is received, categorized, counted, stored, distributed, reported, and backed up. It will also consider the tools already available to staff and volunteers, including computers, tablets, printers, network access, and any existing cloud storage or shared file systems.

## Proposed Approach

The recommended approach is to begin with discovery before making any technical decisions. After the current workflow is understood, the project can identify a minimal first milestone that provides useful improvement without creating unnecessary complexity.

Possible outcomes may include an improved spreadsheet workflow, a lightweight database, a small web application, or another low-cost option. The right solution should be selected after the food bank's actual needs are documented.

## Guiding Principles

- Keep the system simple enough for regular staff and volunteers to use confidently.
- Minimize recurring costs wherever practical.
- Prefer open-source or low-cost tools when they are a good fit.
- Preserve useful parts of the existing workflow.
- Improve accuracy and visibility of available inventory.
- Make backup and recovery part of the design from the beginning.
- Avoid creating a system that depends on one volunteer forever.
- Deliver value incrementally instead of waiting for a large final release.

## Initial Discovery Topics

The discovery meeting should focus on the real workflow used by Project Hope today, including:

- How food donations and purchases are received.
- How inventory quantities are updated.
- How expiration dates, categories, and storage locations are tracked.
- How inventory leaves the food bank.
- What reports are needed for staff, volunteers, donors, grants, or compliance.
- How many people need to use the system at the same time.
- What equipment and network access are available.
- What data must be protected or backed up.

## Near-Term Next Steps

1. Complete the discovery meeting and capture answers to the prepared questions.
2. Document the current workflow from intake through distribution.
3. Identify the highest-effort and highest-risk parts of the current Excel process.
4. Decide on a minimal first milestone.
5. Create implementation issues for the first round of work.

## Personal Note

I am approaching this as a community volunteer who appreciates the role Project Hope plays in Lynden. My intent is to listen first, understand the existing process, and recommend only practical improvements that Project Hope can afford, operate, and maintain over time.
